How to add location stamps to digital photos on Android

Updated June 9, 2026 · About GPS Photo

GPS Photo adds GPS coordinates, address, date, and time directly onto Android photos. Use this guide to create a visible digital photo location stamp for travel, field work, or records.

Step-by-step guide

  1. 1

    Install GPS Photo

    Download GPS Photo from Google Play and open the app. Allow location access when you want the app to add accurate GPS details to a photo stamp.

  2. 2

    Confirm your location details

    Check that the app has the correct GPS coordinates, address context, date, and time before taking the photo.

  3. 3

    Capture the stamped photo

    Take the photo with the location stamp enabled. GPS Photo adds visible location details directly onto the image export.

  4. 4

    Save the photo for documentation

    Save the stamped image to your device so the location evidence remains visible when the photo is shared outside the gallery.

Frequently asked questions

What is a digital photo location stamp?
▾
A digital photo location stamp is visible text or an overlay on an image that shows where and when the photo was taken, such as GPS coordinates, address, date, and time.
Can GPS Photo show latitude and longitude on a photo?
▾
Yes. GPS Photo is designed to place GPS coordinates, including latitude and longitude, directly on Android photos.
Why add a visible location stamp instead of relying on metadata?
▾
A visible stamp stays readable when the photo is shared, copied, or uploaded somewhere that might remove metadata from the file.
